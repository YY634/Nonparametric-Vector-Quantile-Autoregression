This study analyzes the dataset “A dataset of EEG recordings from Alzheimer’s disease, Frontotemporal Dementia, and Healthy subjects”, which is accessible at https://openneuro.org/datasets/ds004504/versions/1.0.8. 
Researchers can apply for the dataset following their standard procedure. 



Before implementing our method, we preprocessed the dataset: 
1: transform .set format to .npy format. The original data is in the form of .set file, and we will transform those into python readable files for subsequent analysis.
2: cleaning the data by removing the missing entries and obtain the channel names (such as F1, F2, O1, O2).
3: extract the (F1, F2) and (O1, O2) subset for subsequent analysis, and also truncate the time series at some feasible length. At this point, we have four groups of time series on R^2--the (F1, F2) signals from FTD patients, the (F1, F2) signals from healthy individuals, the (O1, O2) signals from AD patients, the (O1, O2) signals from healthy individuals. 
4: compute a consensus series within each group using the R package "dtwclust"; then align the time series within the group to the consensus series using the R package "dtw". 
The code for preprocessing is contained in "reproducibility_materials/real_data_analysis/preprocessing_code/"

The above preprocessing steps are not related to our method and take a long time. Therefore, we prepared the datasets that are ready to use. That is, we went through all these preprocessing steps and store the preprocessed datasets under "reproducibility_materials/real_data_analysis/preprocessed_data/". These datasets are named as "dataset_CNFTD.RData", "dataset_FTD.RData", "dataset_CNAD.RData", "dataset_AD.RData", "consensus_series.RData".
